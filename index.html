Basics

What was the British Empire?

The British Empire was a global network of colonies, dominions and territories ruled ‚Äì directly or indirectly ‚Äì from London for more than 400 years. Key facts
scale

Important terms
vocabulary
  • Colony: territory controlled by a foreign power, often with little or no local political freedom.
  • Imperialism: policy of expanding a country's power through colonisation, military force or economic control.
  • Commonwealth: loose association of former British colonies that mostly became independent states.
Think: For people living in a colony, what might ‚Äúempire‚Äù have felt like ‚Äì opportunity, threat, or both?
Speaker notes

Emphasise the size: use the phrase ‚Äúlargest empire in history‚Äù and ‚Äúthe sun never sets‚Äù.

Ask the reflection question quickly to make the class think, then move on.

Over four centuries, Britain built its empire through exploration, trade, wars and treaties ‚Äì often combining economic interests with military force. Peak power

The empire on which the sun never set

Around 1920, the British Empire reached its biggest size ‚Äì ruling over hundreds of millions of people across all inhabited continents. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/history-of-the-british-empire/25479325?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=36715FA9-BB58-4A9B-9921-147757E0A1BD&citationTitle=SlideShare&citationFullTitle=SlideShare&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)

What did this look like?
geography
  • Territories in Canada, the Caribbean and large parts of Africa.
  • Control of India, parts of the Middle East, Australia and New Zealand. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/history-of-the-british-empire/25479325?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=36715FA9-BB58-4A9B-9921-147757E0A1BD&citationTitle=SlideShare&citationFullTitle=SlideShare&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • Important ports and routes: Suez Canal, Singapore, Hong Kong, Cape Town.
This global network made Britain a leading economic, naval and political power ‚Äì but also created strong dependencies and inequalities.

Perspectives

What did life in the empire mean?

Experiences in the British Empire were extremely diverse ‚Äì depending on whether you were a British settler, a local elite, a worker, an enslaved person or part of a resistance movement. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)

Perspectives of power
privilege
  • British officials and settlers often had political rights and economic advantages.
  • Local elites sometimes cooperated with the empire to keep or gain power.
  • Some people saw the empire as a chance for education, trade or a ‚Äúbetter life‚Äù.
Perspectives of oppression
resistance
  • Many people faced racism, exploitation and loss of land and resources. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • Slavery and forced labour were part of the imperial system, especially in earlier centuries. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • Resistance movements and uprisings appeared in many regions, from India to Africa and the Caribbean. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)

Ambivalence

Benefits ‚Äì and at what cost?

The British Empire brought certain forms of infrastructure, administration and trade ‚Äì but often mainly to serve British interests, not local populations. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/history-of-the-british-empire/25479325?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=36715FA9-BB58-4A9B-9921-147757E0A1BD,F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=SlideShare%20+1&citationFullTitle=SlideShare%20+1&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)

Often-mentioned ‚Äúbenefits‚Äù
infrastructure
  • Railways, ports and telegraph lines connected large regions and markets. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/history-of-the-british-empire/25479325?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=36715FA9-BB58-4A9B-9921-147757E0A1BD&citationTitle=SlideShare&citationFullTitle=SlideShare&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • English language spread globally, which today supports international communication.
  • Legal systems, schools and parliaments were introduced in some colonies.
High human costs
exploitation
  • Economic structures often forced colonies to export raw materials and import British products. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • Violent suppression of uprisings, famines, and long-term inequality between groups. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
  • Racist ideologies used to justify domination and unequal rights. [9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1](https://schoolshistory.org.uk/topics/british-empire/teaching-resources-on-the-british-empire/?citationMarker=9F742443-6C92-4C44-BF58-8F5A7C53B6F1&citationId=F168050E-6B87-40B0-BFF3-F3643E3CEC2E&citationTitle=Schools%20History&citationFullTitle=Schools%20History&chatItemId=pGbtP2ptdxMmJNikGzR41)
Critical thought: When we talk about ‚Äúbenefits‚Äù, we must always ask: benefits for whom? ‚Äì and who paid the price?
Speaker notes

Show that both sides are discussed ‚Äì but that the ‚Äúbenefits‚Äù were not neutral.

Use simple, clear English and maybe one short example (e.g. railways in India).

Turning point

World wars and the end of the empire

The two World Wars weakened Britain economically and politically ‚Äì at the same time, they strengthened independence movements in many colonies.
